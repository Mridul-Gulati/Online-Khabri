New Delhi: India has sharply reacted to a Washington Post article claiming that a member of its intelligence agency, the Research and Analysis Wing (RAW), was involved in a plot to assassinate a Khalistani separatist in the United States. The Indian government has denounced the report as "unwarranted and unsubstantial."

## RAW Member Allegedly Involved in Assassination Plan

The Washington Post report alleges that RAW member Vikram Yadav allegedly directed a hit squad to eliminate Gurpatwant Singh Pannun, a Khalistani separatist based in the United States. According to the report, US intelligence agencies believe that the assassination attempt was approved by the then RAW chief, Samant Goel. Yadav was reportedly reassigned to the Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF) after the failed operation was uncovered last year.

## India Investigating US Security Concerns

India has emphasized that it is already investigating the security concerns raised by the US government regarding networks of organized criminals and terrorists. Spokesperson Randhir Jaiswal tweeted, "Speculative and irresponsible comments on this ongoing investigation are not helpful."

## White House: India Taking Allegations Seriously

The White House has stated that India is taking the allegations of the assassination plot seriously. However, it declined to comment on the ongoing FBI probe and the criminal case filed by the Department of Justice.

## Pannun: Key Figure in Khalistan Movement

Gurpatwant Singh Pannun is a prominent figure in the Khalistan movement, which advocates for a separate Sikh state. The Indian government has designated Pannun as a terrorist. The reported assassination plot coincides with the fatal shooting of Khalistani terrorist Hardeep Singh Nijjar in Canada last year, which has also been linked to Yadav.
