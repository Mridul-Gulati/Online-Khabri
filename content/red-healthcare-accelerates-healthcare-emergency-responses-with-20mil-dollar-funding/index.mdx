Hyderabad, India - RED.Health, the game-changer in medical emergency response, has secured $20 million in a Series B funding round led by Jungle Ventures. The investment will fuel the startup's mission to revolutionize emergency healthcare in India.

## Bridging the Gap

RED.Health is tackling the challenge of unreliable and delayed emergency medical services in India. Their AI-powered platform seamlessly connects patients with certified ambulances within an astonishing eight minutes. The company's triage system ensures the right hospital is selected, with vital information instantly shared with responders.

## Innovation and Expansion

The fresh funding will empower RED.Health to expand its innovation pipeline. They aim to develop cutting-edge technologies, such as an auto-dispatch system that minimizes errors and optimizes response time. The startup is also setting its sights on introducing subscription-based models, providing accessible and transparent medical assistance.

> "This investment is a testament to our unwavering commitment to transforming emergency healthcare," exclaimed RED.Health founder and CEO Prabhdeep Singh. "We envision a future where every Indian has access to timely, reliable, and standardized emergency care."

## Nationwide Reach

RED.Health currently operates in 20 cities across India. With this funding, the company plans to expand to 40 cities, partnering with over 500 hospitals. They will also enhance their fleet of 450 managed ambulances and tap into a network of 6,000 Saathi road ambulances.

## Partners in Progress

Jungle Ventures partner-healthcare Seemant Jauhari expressed his excitement about collaborating with RED.Health. "Together, we are building a comprehensive platform that will provide families with peace of mind, knowing that they can rely on immediate and efficient emergency care."

## A Beacon of Hope

RED.Health's unwavering commitment to innovation and accessibility is a beacon of hope for India's healthcare landscape. As the company expands its reach, it promises to redefine emergency response, delivering a lifeline to those in critical need.
